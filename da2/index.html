Star Collector

  • Originality: The originality of my game is the two elements of dodging blocks and collecting stars at the same time.
  • Technical Merit: I used a new phaser project and re-structured the assignments directories and code structure so that it is more object oriented, the boxes and stars are both randomized and on a timer object, the text on the title screen it on a timer, I used a custom bitmap font for all of the text, and there is also a sprinting mechanic.
  • Prototype Postmortem: This project taught me how to use Phaser3 and Javascript with a level of object-orientation. The hardest part was switching from Phaser2 to Phaser3, I had previously made the game in Phaser2 but after seeings the amount of support Phaser3 had I decided to switch over. The easiest part was the conceptual idea of the game. I find it easier to prototype digitally than I do physically.
  • Prototype Assets: All of the assets used were in phaser's default asset kit from its tutorials.

  • Prototype Closest Other Game: All of the code was made by me, I did use the basic physics example from the Phaser 3 tutorials. The closest game this would resemble would be a vertical shoot-em-up game without the scrolls and vertical movement (in a sense that you are dodging things and colecting things, there is just no vertical movement besides jumping).
  • High Concept: You are an alien stuck on planet earth trying to dodge all of the boxes that the humans are dropping on you whilst collecting stars to refuel your spaceship. You lose by being hit by a box and your score increases per star you collect.
  • Theme: Field not used for this assignment.
  • Mandated Variety: I used a side view (left-to-right) platformer input style for this game, there is a degree of randomness in where the boxes and stars drop, the genre would be a casual platformer, the type of play-style is a run, dodge, and collect play-style.
  • Prototype Goal: The game mechanics that are being prototyped in this game are the movement (walking, sprinting, jumping), and the random box/star spawning.
  • Player Experience Goals: The players should experience a feeling of dread and excitement while playing this game. Dread from the boxes and excitement from the scoring. There is little to no element of competitiveness in this game.
  • Gameplay: The player can move left and right (using the left and right arrows), walk or sprint (by holding shift), and jump (by pressing the spacebar). Players start at a score of 0 and gain 100 points each time they grab a star (by walking into them) that falls from the sky, they will lose the game if they hit a green box which falls from the sky aswell.   • Strategies: A strategy that I tested and found effective was to stay in a small area and make small and basic movements to avoid the blocks instead of running the length of the map.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: The player is an alien who has ran out of fuel and landed on the earth, his space ship is powered by stars and he is in the rare event of a day-time star-shower on the earth so he is trying to collect as many stars as he can to fuel his ship. The humans on the earth are trying to stop him by dropping green boxes on him so they can use the star themselves as a substitute for oil in their economy. This story is unique because not only does it tell a tale about an alien that has lost his way in the universe, but also shows the struggle for non-renewable resources on the earth. The only feasable dramatic-tension (it isn't much) is the urgency to collect fuel for your ship.
  • Target Audience: The target-audience this is trying to reach is for young children.
  • Play Time: The play time is all dependent on the player's skill, in theory this could go on forever, but the average playtime is 1-5 minutes.
